A short description of a p-values:

The p-value describes how likely the it is for the difference in mean values between two groups to be due to random fluctuations.
As it is a probability the p-value is a number between zero and one. The lower the p-value the less likely it is that the differences
between the different groups are due to randomnes. It is usual to require the p-value to be less than 0.05 to say that there is a
statistical significant difference between the groups.
